BULLISH / EUPHORIC QUOTES BEFORE THE COVID CRASH (Feb 19, 2020 S&P 500 Peak)
================================================================================
All quotes are real and verifiable. Sources provided.

================================================================================
SECTION 1: WALL STREET YEAR-END 2020 S&P 500 TARGETS (Issued Dec 2019)
================================================================================

The S&P 500 closed 2019 at ~3,230. It peaked at ~3,386 on Feb 19, 2020.
It crashed to ~2,237 by March 23, 2020 -- a 34% decline.

GOLDMAN SACHS -- David Kostin (Chief U.S. Equity Strategist)
  Year-end 2020 target: 3,400
  "The dovish Fed pivot has driven the equity market rally in 2019, and we
   expect low interest rates will continue to support above-average valuations
   going forward."
  -- December 2019
  Source: Yahoo Finance / Goldman Sachs 2020 outlook

JPMORGAN -- Dubravko Lakos-Bujas (Head of U.S. Equity Strategy)
  Year-end 2020 target: 3,400
  Said the S&P 500 would rise to 3,400 on "global cycle recovery, partial
  trade deal, pro-growth election-year rhetoric and neutral investor positioning."
  -- December 2019 (appeared on CNBC Dec 12, 2019)
  Source: CNBC, Yahoo Finance

DEUTSCHE BANK -- Binky Chadha (Chief Strategist)
  Year-end 2020 target: 3,250
  One of the more bullish prognosticators on Wall Street that year.
  -- December 2019
  Source: Yahoo Finance

LPL FINANCIAL
  Base case: S&P 500 earnings per share rising to $175 in 2020 (from ~$165).
  Stocks heading "marginally higher" in 2020.
  -- December 2019
  Source: LPL Financial Research

MORGAN STANLEY -- Mike Wilson & UBS -- Francois Trahan
  Year-end 2020 target: 3,000 (the BEARISH call on the Street)
  Even the bears only expected a modest pullback.
  -- December 2019
  Source: CNBC, Yahoo Finance

WALL STREET CONSENSUS:
  Median strategist target: 3,325 (implied ~7% gain)
  Average target: 3,272 (implied ~5% gain)
  Most bullish target on the Street: 3,600
  Most bearish target on the Street: 3,000
  NOBODY predicted a crash. The "worst case" was essentially flat.
  Source: CNBC (Nov 23, 2019) -- "Wall Street's stock forecasters see just a 5% gain in 2020"

SECTION 2: TRUMP ADMINISTRATION -- STATE OF THE UNION (February 4, 2020)
================================================================================
(15 days before the S&P 500 all-time high)

DONALD TRUMP -- State of the Union Address, February 4, 2020:

  "I am thrilled to report to you tonight that our economy is the best it
   has ever been."

  "Jobs are booming, incomes are soaring, poverty is plummeting, crime is
   falling, confidence is surging, and our country is thriving and highly
   respected again."

  "The unemployment rate is the lowest in over half a century."

  "Very incredibly, the average unemployment rate under my administration is
   lower than any administration in the history of our country."

  "The U.S. economy is growing almost twice as fast today as when I took
   office. And we are considered, far and away, the hottest economy anywhere
   in the world."

  "Since my election, we have created 7 million new jobs -- 5 million more
   than government experts projected during the previous administration."

  Source: White House Archives transcript
  https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefings-statements/remarks-president-trump-state-union-address-3/

SECTION 3: LARRY KUDLOW -- WHITE HOUSE ECONOMIC ADVISER
================================================================================

LARRY KUDLOW -- February 25, 2020 (on CNBC's "The Exchange"):
(6 days AFTER the S&P 500 peak, as market was just starting to crack)

  "We have contained this. I won't say [it's] airtight, but it's pretty
   close to airtight."

  Said the outbreak is a "human tragedy" but it will likely NOT be an
  "economic tragedy."

  Source: CNBC -- "Larry Kudlow says US has contained the coronavirus and the
  economy is holding up nicely" (Feb 25, 2020)
  https://www.cnbc.com/2020/02/25/larry-kudlow-says-us-has-contained-the-coronavirus-and-the-economy-is-holding-up-nicely.html

LARRY KUDLOW -- Late February 2020:

  Told investors they should "seriously consider" buying the dip.
  "Stocks look pretty cheap to me."

  Source: CNBC

NOTE: On the SAME DAY as Kudlow's "contained" comment (Feb 25), CDC official
Nancy Messonnier told reporters: "It's not a question of if but rather a
question of when and how many people in this country will have severe illness."

KUDLOW LATER ADMITTED HE WAS WRONG:
  On March 23, 2020, Kudlow admitted he was wrong about the virus being
  "contained."
  Source: CNBC -- "Trump advisor Larry Kudlow admits he was wrong about
  coronavirus being 'contained'" (March 23, 2020)
  https://www.cnbc.com/2020/03/23/coronavirus-kudlow-admits-he-was-wrong-about-disease-being-contained.html

SECTION 4: MORGAN STANLEY -- "CORRECTION SHOULD REMAIN LIMITED"
================================================================================

MORGAN STANLEY -- Mike Wilson (Chief U.S. Equities Strategist)
  ~February 10, 2020:

  Wrote that the S&P 500's correction of 3.5% was relatively small and
  "should remain limited" before stocks resume their upward climb.

  Morgan Stanley said US stocks could jump more than 5% in the first half
  of 2020 after tumbling on coronavirus concerns.

  Source: Business Insider (Feb 10, 2020) -- "MORGAN STANLEY: Here are 5
  reasons why US stocks will shrug off coronavirus and jump 5% by June"

SECTION 5: JIM CRAMER -- CNBC
================================================================================

JIM CRAMER -- February 13, 2020 (CNBC):
  "Buy high and sell higher is working here. Buy high and not selling is
   the best."
  Source: CNBC -- "The coronavirus outbreak has made 'buy high and sell higher'
  a winning playbook, Jim Cramer says" (Feb 13, 2020)

JIM CRAMER -- February 24, 2020 (Mad Money):
  "The great opportunities -- and, yes, I'm willing to use that word -- come
   from the obliteration of the futures."
  Positioned the selloff as a buying opportunity.
  Source: CNBC -- Jim Cramer's Mad Money recap (Feb 24, 2020)

JIM CRAMER -- February 27, 2020:
  Recommended 10 tech stocks to buy, saying they "don't need China, they
  don't need the Fed, they don't need enterprise spending and they don't
  need us to stop the coronavirus."
  Source: CNBC (Feb 27, 2020)

NOTE: By March 17, 2020, Cramer completely reversed: "It's really the
opposite of buy the dips." The S&P 500 had fallen ~30% by then.

SECTION 6: BROADER WALL STREET DISMISSAL OF COVID RISK
================================================================================

WORLD ECONOMIC FORUM -- Global Risk Report 2020 (Published January 15, 2020):
  Infectious diseases ranked TENTH in terms of impact.
  Considered "quite unlikely."
  Attention was focused on traditional business risks and environmental issues.
  Source: WEF Global Risk Report 2020

CORPORATE AMERICA:
  Corporate managers and analysts only started paying attention to the novel
  coronavirus AFTER January 20, 2020. The first international corporate
  conference call discussing coronavirus occurred on January 22, 2020.
  Source: Academic research (Oxford Academic / RAPS)

CNBC -- February 8, 2020:
  CNBC headline: "Buy these stocks on the dip related to the coronavirus,
  Wall Street analysts say"
  Source: https://www.cnbc.com/2020/02/08/these-stocks-are-safe-from-the-impact-of-coronavirus.html

SECTION 7: MARKET CONTEXT / WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENED
================================================================================

  - S&P 500 gained 15.8% in the six months leading to the Feb 19, 2020 peak
  - The market hit a SERIES of new all-time highs in January and February 2020
  - From Feb 19 peak to March 23 bottom: S&P 500 declined ~34%
  - Circuit breakers halted trading THREE TIMES in one week (March 9, 12, 16)
  - On March 16 alone, the Dow dropped 12.9% and S&P 500 dropped 12%
  - It was the fastest correction from all-time high in market history,
    taking merely SIX DAYS (Feb 24-28) to enter correction territory
  - The 2020 crash followed a decade of economic prosperity, with global
    unemployment at historic lows, contributing to extreme complacency
